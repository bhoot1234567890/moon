Yes, each of the **10 Mandalas** of the **Rigveda** is often associated with a general theme or purpose, though these names aren't as formalized as titles for entire books. Instead, each Mandala is usually named after the **sages** (Rishis) who composed the hymns, or after the deities to whom the hymns are dedicated.

Here’s a brief look at how the Mandalas of the Rigveda are commonly referred to:

### **1. Mandala 1 - "Agni Mandala"**

* **Theme**: Primarily dedicated to **Agni**, the Fire God, but also to other deities like **Indra** and **Varuna**.
* **Sage(s)**: Composed by **Vishwamitra**, **Vamadeva**, and other early rishis.

### **2. Mandala 2 - "Brahmanaspati Mandala"**

* **Theme**: This Mandala focuses on **Brahmanaspati** (the deity of prayer and devotion), **Indra**, **Agni**, and **Soma**.
* **Sage(s)**: It includes hymns by **Gritsamada**, **Vishwamitra**, and **Vamadeva**.

### **3. Mandala 3 - "Indra Mandala"**

* **Theme**: Dedicated primarily to **Indra**, the god of thunder and battle, along with **Agni** and **Soma**.
* **Sage(s)**: Composed by **Vishwamitra**, **Vamadeva**, and others.

### **4. Mandala 4 - "Vishnu Mandala"**

* **Theme**: While the primary focus is on **Indra** and **Agni**, this Mandala also includes the **Vishnu** hymns and his association with the cosmic order.
* **Sage(s)**: The hymns were composed by sages like **Vishwamitra**, **Vamadeva**, and **Atri**.

### **5. Mandala 5 - "Soma Mandala"**

* **Theme**: Dedicated to **Soma**, the sacred drink and associated deity. This Mandala explores the ritual significance of Soma.
* **Sage(s)**: Composed by **Atri**, **Vishwamitra**, **Vamadeva**, and others.

### **6. Mandala 6 - "Agni-Varuna Mandala"**

* **Theme**: Primarily dedicated to **Agni** and **Varuna**, with a focus on the powers of law and cosmic order.
* **Sage(s)**: Composed by **Vishwamitra**, **Vamadeva**, and others.

### **7. Mandala 7 - "Indra Mandala"**

* **Theme**: Focused primarily on **Indra**, as well as **Agni** and **Soma**. Many hymns in this Mandala are directed toward the warrior-god **Indra**.
* **Sage(s)**: Composed by **Vishwamitra** and others.

### **8. Mandala 8 - "Soma Mandala"**

* **Theme**: This Mandala deals with **Soma**, focusing on its use in rituals and the significance of Soma in Vedic practice.
* **Sage(s)**: Composed by **Atri**, **Vishwamitra**, and others.

### **9. Mandala 9 - "Soma Mandala"**

* **Theme**: Like Mandala 8, it is heavily dedicated to **Soma**, but it also includes hymns related to the ritual use of Soma and its power.
* **Sage(s)**: Composed by **Atri**, **Vishwamitra**, and other sages.

### **10. Mandala 10 - "Philosophical Mandala"**

* **Theme**: This Mandala includes the most philosophical hymns, such as the **Purusha Sukta**, which speaks of the cosmic sacrifice and the creation of the universe. It explores ideas about the nature of the cosmos, creation, and the divine.
* **Sage(s)**: Composed by various rishis, including **Bharadvaja** and **Vishwamitra**.

### Summary of Key Themes Across the Mandalas:

* **Mandalas 1–5**: Primarily focus on ritualistic hymns dedicated to **Indra**, **Agni**, **Soma**, and other deities.
* **Mandalas 6–9**: Continue the themes of sacrifice and divine praise but also incorporate more philosophical and cosmological musings.
* **Mandala 10**: The most philosophical, focusing on creation, cosmic order, and the nature of the divine.

### The Naming System:

In general, these Mandalas are often referred to by the central deity or theme they deal with, like **Agni Mandala**, **Indra Mandala**, or **Soma Mandala**, but they don’t have formal, standardized names like "Book 1" or "Book 2." The name generally reflects the most prominent deity or subject matter in that Mandala.
